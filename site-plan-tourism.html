Home page

Côte d'Ivoire (also called Côte d'Ivoire in English), a tropical country in southern West Africa on the Gulf of Guinea (North Atlantic Ocean), bordered to the north by Mali and Burkina Faso, to to the west by Liberia and Guinea, and to the east by Ghana. Côte d'Ivoire has an area of 322,463 km², with a population of 26 million. The languages spoken are French (official); and the languages of the five main linguistic groups, the Diula (Dioula), the Baule (Baoulé), the Dan, the Anyin and the Senari. About 40% of the population are Muslims, 34% are Christians and 25% practice traditional religions (animists).
